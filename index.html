Something for every playstyle (seriously)

The best way I can describe Doomfall as a player: it never tells you “this is the only correct way to play”. It gives you a toolbox, and you decide what kind of run you want.

  • Want vanilla difficulty but a world packed with more monsters? You can.
  • Want to keep everything vanilla and only inject new unique bosses here and there? You can.
  • Want to tweak combat difficulty without touching density at all? You can.
  • Want everything set to vanilla and just enjoy occasional “oh no, it’s a boss” moments? Totally doable.

Nothing is mandatory. Doomfall doesn’t force a preset experience — it lets you shape MMMerge exactly the way you like.

The 3 Doomfall settings (independent systems)

Doomfall splits difficulty into three separate knobs you can adjust independently. This is what makes it feel so flexible: you’re not locked into “hard mode = everything goes crazy”.

1) Difficulty

This controls the overall combat intensity in Might and Magic Merge: how punishing fights feel, how dangerous enemies are, and how much pressure you’re under.

Low presets (Piece of Cake, Very Easy, Easy) are perfect if you’re new to MMMerge, or you just want a relaxed adventure. Higher values ramp up hard: enemies hit harder, survive longer, and fights turn into real tests — the “jump straight into the fire” type of scaling.

Key point: you can change Difficulty without touching Density or Bosses.

2) Density

Density is all about how many monsters populate the world — not how strong they are. Vanilla density keeps the “classic MMMerge feel”. Higher density turns maps into real battlefields.

The crazy part? It can scale to massive encounters with hundreds of enemies — even up to ~1000 active monsters on a single map — while keeping the original monster distribution intact.

If you want more action but don’t want spongey enemies, Density is your best friend.

3) Boss

This setting controls the appearance of additional unique bosses. You can keep Difficulty and Density at pure vanilla and enable only Bosses — and suddenly the world has these elite encounters that break the routine in the best way.

Bosses are built from existing monsters, then enhanced with special abilities or elite combos, so they feel like “this map’s monsters, but evolved”, not random replacements.

Total freedom (mix or isolate)

  • Change only Density.
  • Change only Difficulty.
  • Enable only Bosses.
  • Or combine all three for full Doomfall chaos.

Beginner-friendly by design

Doomfall can get extreme, but it’s also built to be playable for newcomers. The low presets (Piece of Cake, Very Easy, Easy) exist for a reason: you can learn the systems at your pace, then gradually push one slider at a time.

Core philosophy

Doomfall’s core idea is simple: difficulty in MMMerge should be customizable. You decide how far the game goes — not fixed presets, not engine limits.

What Doomfall actually adds (player breakdown)

Full difficulty control

Difficulty scaling is modular and independent from monster density. You can fine-tune overall challenge, damage pressure, survivability, and combat intensity, which makes progression feel smooth — from beginner-friendly runs to real endgame-level brutality, even on familiar MMMerge maps.

Extreme monster density (engine-aware)

Doomfall can push density far beyond vanilla. The system is engineered to stay stable: intelligent pseudo-spawning, preservation of the original monster distribution, and filtering out unstable entities (like town NPCs/guards) so the game doesn’t implode.

Dynamic boss system (50+ extra bosses)

Doomfall introduces more than 50 additional bosses, generated dynamically from the map’s actual monster population. They’re not random replacements:

  • Picked from real map populations (so they fit the area)
  • Minority monster types are avoided to keep things fair and readable
  • Spawned safely after map load

Boss skills & affixes (the “oh no” moments)

Bosses can roll powerful abilities that change how fights play out — things like: Adamantite, Berserker, Blinker, Voodoo, Diamondskin, Immolator, Horror, Lifedrinker, Mana Vampire, Petrifier, Plagueborn, Summoner, Terror, Void Stalker, Venomous, Wrath Animator, and more.

Elite bosses (rare, memorable, mean)

Sometimes a boss becomes Elite: multiple synergistic abilities, extra durability, and a fight that can completely hijack your plan for the next five minutes (in a good way). Examples include: Destroyer, Gladiator, Phantom, War Bringer, Spectral Overlord, Colossus, Bastion.

Engine-safe & multiplayer-safe design

Even with ridiculous settings, Doomfall prioritizes stability in the MMMerge engine: delayed safe spawning, locks to prevent duplicate bosses, full New Game reset handling, dynamic naming to avoid conflicts, and multiplayer-safe behavior.

FAQ — Doomfall for MMMerge

Can I max all three settings?

Yes — you can push Difficulty, Density, and Bosses to the maximum. Just don’t be surprised if the result is completely unhinged: the game can become genuinely overwhelming. (It’s fun… but it’s not subtle.)

Is Doomfall compatible with multiplayer?

Yes. Doomfall is designed with multiplayer in mind. Not every edge case is guaranteed to be solved forever, but the foundation is multiplayer-safe, and patches can address anything weird that shows up in the wild.

Why are new classes included?

Because the Extra Class Pack works naturally with the base MMMerge experience and gives a complete “ready-to-play” setup. It’s included so players can get the full package in one install — without forcing those classes into Doomfall’s difficulty systems.

Example: A Monk will automatically autorank Dodge and Unarmed while playing on Jadame or Enroth — but not on Antagarich, where the proper trainers already exist.

FAQ (player answers)

Can I change the hotkeys for special skills?

Yes. Go to Scripts > General, open zzz_HOTN-initialize, and modify the key values (you must enter a number).

Is it compatible with multiplayer?

Yes — multiplayer is fully playable. One detail that can surprise people: summons remain alive if their owner dies. It’s intentional and helps avoid rare but nasty engine issues when entities get cleaned up on death. In practice: it keeps the run stable for everyone.

Is it compatible with MAW?

No. Too many errors happen in-game when combining them, so it’s not recommended.

Do you plan to make more mods?

Yes. The original plan was a larger project called Heroes of the Nexus. That full version is planned for a future release, so this module was published as a standalone, lightweight pack that stays easy to integrate into the base MMMerge setup.

Why is Dodge different? Is it stronger?

Dodge was adjusted so it can avoid all attacks regardless of skill level — with a low chance at low ranks (around 5%), scaling up to about 25% at 20 skill points. The change exists because the vanilla behavior is bugged, and several new classes rely heavily on Dodge.
